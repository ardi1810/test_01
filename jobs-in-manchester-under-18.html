Jobs For Under 18S In Manchester: Opportunities And Tips

Introduction

Are you under 18 and looking for a job in Manchester? Whether you want to earn some extra cash, gain work experience, or support your studies, there are plenty of opportunities out there. In this article, we'll explore some of the best jobs for under 18s in Manchester, as well as some tips on how to find and ace them.

Types of Jobs for Under 18s in Manchester

Retail and Hospitality

The retail and hospitality sectors are among the most popular for under 18s in Manchester. You can work in shops, cafes, restaurants, hotels, and other venues, doing tasks such as serving customers, helping with stock, cleaning, and cash handling. Some of the big names that hire under 18s in Manchester include Tesco, McDonald's, Costa Coffee, and Premier Inn.

Delivery and Courier

Another option for under 18s in Manchester is to work as a delivery or courier driver. You can use your own bike, scooter, or car (if you have one), or work for companies such as Deliveroo, Uber Eats, or Hermes. You'll need to have a valid driving license, insurance, and a good knowledge of the local area.

Online and Freelance

If you prefer to work from home or on your own schedule, you can look for online or freelance jobs. These can include writing, editing, designing, coding, or tutoring, among others. You can find such jobs on platforms such as Upwork, Fiverr, or TaskRabbit, or by reaching out to local businesses or individuals who need your skills.

Tips for Finding and Applying for Jobs as an Under 18 in Manchester

Check the Requirements

Before applying for any job, make sure you meet the age, education, and other requirements. Some jobs may require you to be at least 16 or 18, to have a specific grade or qualification, or to have a work permit or visa if you're not a UK citizen. Check the job description or website carefully, and don't hesitate to ask the employer if you're unsure.

Prepare a CV and Cover Letter

Even if you don't have much work experience, you can still create a CV and cover letter that showcase your skills, achievements, and personality. Include your education, hobbies, volunteer work, and any other relevant activities that demonstrate your motivation, reliability, and communication skills. Keep your CV and cover letter concise, clear, and error-free.

Apply Early and Follow Up

When you find a job that interests you, apply as soon as possible. Many employers receive hundreds of applications and may close the vacancy early if they find a suitable candidate. Make sure you provide all the required information, such as your availability, contact details, and references. After you apply, don't be afraid to follow up with the employer by email or phone, to show your interest and enthusiasm.

Be Professional and Positive

Whether you apply online, in person, or by phone, always present yourself in a professional and positive manner. Dress appropriately, speak clearly and politely, and show that you're eager to learn and contribute. Don't be discouraged if you don't get the job at first; ask for feedback and keep improving your skills and attitude.

Conclusion

There are many jobs for under 18s in Manchester, from retail and hospitality to delivery and online work. By following our tips and being proactive, you can increase your chances of finding and landing a job that suits your needs and interests. Good luck!
