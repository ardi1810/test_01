The Job Description of a CNA

A CNA is responsible for providing basic care to patients in various healthcare settings. This includes assisting patients with activities of daily living, such as bathing, dressing, and grooming. CNAs also take vital signs, record patient information, and assist with transferring patients from one place to another.

The Requirements for Becoming a CNA

To become a CNA in Gulfport, MS, you must complete a state-approved training program and pass a competency exam. The training program typically lasts between 4-12 weeks and covers topics such as patient care, infection control, and communication skills.

The Benefits of Being a CNA

CNAs enjoy several benefits, including job security, flexible scheduling, and the opportunity to make a difference in people's lives. They also have the option to further their education and advance their careers in the healthcare industry.
