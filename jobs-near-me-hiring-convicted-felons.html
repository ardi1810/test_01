Introduction

Are you a convicted felon looking for a job? It can be tough to find employment when you have a criminal record, but there are companies out there that are willing to give ex-offenders a second chance. In this article, we will discuss some of the jobs near you that hire convicted felons.

Why Hire Convicted Felons?

Hiring convicted felons is a win-win situation for both the employer and the ex-offender. By giving someone a second chance, the employer is helping to reduce recidivism and giving someone the opportunity to turn their life around. For the ex-offender, a job can provide stability, a sense of purpose, and a way to provide for themselves and their family.

Jobs Near Me

There are many jobs near you that hire convicted felons. Some examples include:

Construction

Construction is a great industry for ex-offenders because it is in high demand and typically pays well. Many construction companies are willing to hire felons for positions such as laborers, carpenters, and electricians.

Trucking

Trucking is another industry that is open to hiring ex-offenders. Commercial truck drivers are in high demand, and many companies are willing to train and hire felons for these positions.

Manufacturing

Manufacturing is another industry that is open to hiring ex-offenders. Many companies have a high demand for workers and are willing to overlook a criminal record for the right candidate.

Tips for Job Seekers

If you are a convicted felon looking for a job, there are some tips that can help you in your job search.

Be Honest

It is important to be upfront and honest about your criminal record. Many employers will do a background check, so it is better to be honest from the start.

Get Training

Getting training or certification in a specific field can make you a more desirable candidate. Look for programs in your area that offer training for the industries that are open to hiring ex-offenders.

Network

Networking is important in any job search, and it can be especially helpful for ex-offenders. Reach out to organizations that help ex-offenders find employment, and attend job fairs and networking events in your area.

Conclusion

Finding a job as a convicted felon can be challenging, but it is not impossible. Many companies are willing to give ex-offenders a second chance, and there are industries that are open to hiring felons. By being honest, getting training, and networking, you can increase your chances of finding a job near you.
